# AI PR Engine Prompts Reference

These are the 6 core prompts from The AI Break article that power the PR Engine:

## Prompt #1: The Story Miner
Extracts 5-8 newsworthy angles and ranks by media appeal:
- Timeliness (current trend connection)
- Impact (affects important groups)
- Novelty (genuinely new/surprising)
- Human interest (emotional element)

Story formats: Trend piece, Data story, Founder journey, Customer impact, Contrarian take, Milestone

## Prompt #2: The Press Release Architect
AP-style press release structure:
- Headline: Strong, factual, no hype (12 words max)
- Subheadline: One sentence adding context
- Lead: Most important fact first, tweetable
- Body: Specific numbers, dates, data
- Quote: Conversational, adds insight
- Boilerplate: 2-3 sentence company description

Rules: No exclamation marks, no superlatives, under 500 words, third person

## Prompt #3: The Media Matchmaker
Build targeted journalist lists by tier:
- A-list: Covered nearly identical stories recently
- B-list: Cover the industry but different angles
- C-list: Generalist reporters who occasionally cover the space

Search strings for finding journalists on Google, X, LinkedIn

## Prompt #4: The Pitch Crafter
Three templates for different journalist tiers:
- Template A: Reference their article, connect to their coverage (120 words)
- Template B: Lead with newsworthy fact (130 words)
- Template C: Lead with trend angle (100 words)

Rules: No "hope this finds you well", get to the point, personalize every pitch

## Prompt #5: The Follow-Up Engine
3-touch sequence with specific timing:
- Follow-up #1 (3-4 days): Add NEW information
- Follow-up #2 (5-7 days): Offer different angle
- Follow-up #3 (7-10 days): Graceful close

Never say "just following up", each email adds value

## Prompt #6: The PR Calendar Builder
90-day editorial calendar with:
- 8-12 pitch opportunities
- Mix of company news (3-4), trend-jacking (2-3), thought leadership (2-3), data stories (1-2)
- Monthly rhythm and always-on tactics
- Rapid response protocol for breaking news

## Pro Tips
- Start with trade press, not major outlets
- Build relationships before you need them
- Re-run story mining quarterly
- Repurpose everything
- Track what works (journalist, publication, subject line, response)
