Battle-tested scripts for phone calls, coffee meetings, and in-person recruiting conversations.

## Opening Framework: The 3-Minute Hook

### For Cold Calls
> "Hi {{NAME}}, this is Ryan Rodenbeck from Spyglass Realty. I know you're busy, so I'll be quick — I've been looking at your production numbers and they're really impressive. {{DEAL_COUNT}} transactions at ${{VOLUME}} in this market takes serious skill. I'm curious: are you happy with what you're keeping after fees? Because I think there's a conversation worth having. Got 2 minutes?"

### For Coffee/In-Person
> "Thanks for meeting me. I'm not going to give you a sales pitch — you're too smart for that, and frankly, the numbers should speak for themselves. Let me show you three things, and then you tell me if any of it matters to you."

**The Three Things:**
1. The commission comparison (pull up Calculator)
2. The tech stack demo (show Mission Control on phone)
3. The trajectory (179 agents, $400M+ volume, going to 300)

> At your volume of ${{VOLUME}}, that means you'd pay approximately ${{SPYGLASS_COST}} per year total. You're currently paying roughly ${{COMPETITOR_COST}}. That's ${{SAVINGS}} difference — straight into your pocket.

> And here's the thing: as your volume grows, the gap gets even bigger. Once you hit cap, every additional deal costs you $285 instead of 25-30% of the commission."

## Competitor-Specific Angles

### vs. Keller Williams
> "KW's 64/30/6 split means you're losing 36 cents of every dollar before cap — and then there's the 6% franchise fee that NEVER goes away, even after cap. At Spyglass, once you hit our $12K cap, your only cost is $285 per deal. For a $10M producer, that's roughly $15-20K more per year in your pocket. And you don't have to sit through Mega Agent meetings to get it."

**Key weakness:** The 6% royalty is permanent (never caps). Many KW agents don't realize this.

### vs. Compass
> "Compass is a great brand. But their split structure — even at the high end — takes a bigger bite than you might think, especially with the marketing and technology fees that get added on. And that $450M they spent on technology? Most of their agents say they still use the same tools everyone else uses. We built our own tech stack — Mission Control — and it's actually useful. And included."

**Key weakness:** Compass's stock price collapse, layoffs, and focus on luxury (most agents don't benefit). Tech platform hasn't delivered on promises.

### vs. eXp Realty
> "eXp's virtual model works for some people. But if you want an actual office, a training room, local market expertise, and a team around you — there's a difference between a cloud brokerage and a real one. Our split is comparable (80/20 vs 85/15), but we cap lower ($12K vs $16K), and you're not losing 5% to stock that's been underperforming."

**Key weakness:** Virtual-only model, revenue share complexity makes real costs unclear, stock volatility.

### vs. Realty Austin
> "Realty Austin is a solid local shop. But their 70/30 split and $24K cap means you're paying significantly more than you need to. For a $5M producer, that's roughly $8-10K more per year than you'd pay at Spyglass. Same Austin market knowledge, same local presence — just a better deal."

**Key weakness:** Higher split and cap, less tech differentiation, smaller footprint for referral network.

### vs. Coldwell Banker / RE/MAX
> "The franchise model served its purpose 20 years ago. Today, the brand recognition that justified those fees? Consumers search on Zillow, Redfin, and Google — not Coldwell Banker's website. You're paying premium fees for a brand that doesn't drive your business. At Spyglass, every dollar goes to tools and support that directly help you close deals."

## Handling the Top 15 Objections

### 1. "I'm happy where I am"
> "That's great — I'm glad to hear it. Most successful agents are content. But I've found that 'happy' sometimes means 'comfortable' — and comfort can be expensive. If you found out you were leaving $15K on the table every year, would that change anything? Let me just show you the numbers — no obligation."

### 2. "I don't want to lose my sphere/clients"
> "Your clients follow YOU, not your brokerage. Think about it — when was the last time a buyer called Keller Williams and asked for a random agent? They called you. Your sphere, your relationships, your reputation — all of that comes with you. We make the transition seamless."

### 3. "I've heard bad things about (insert concern)"
> "I appreciate you being straight with me. What specifically did you hear? I'd rather address it directly than have it linger. We're an open book."

### 4. "I need to think about it"
> "Absolutely — this is a big decision and you should think about it. Let me send you our Commission Calculator so you can run your own numbers privately. No pressure. If the math makes sense, let's talk again. If it doesn't, no hard feelings."

### 5. "The split doesn't matter — it's about the leads"
> "Fair point. So let me share two things: First, we're the #1 converting Zillow team in Austin, and we own MoveToAustin.org which generates 3-5K inbound visitors per month. Second, with the money you save on commission, you can fund your own lead generation and keep 100% of those leads forever. Which would you rather have — leads your brokerage controls, or leads YOU control?"

### 6. "I have a team at my current brokerage"
> "We love teams. Several of our top producers brought their teams with us. We can structure it however works best — same team split, same operations, just a much better house split on top."

### 7. "What about training/support?"
> "We have a new 50-person training room, weekly masterminds, and our tech stack IS the training. Pulse gives you market intelligence that makes you look like a genius. Our CMA Builder does the heavy lifting on presentations. And you have direct access to me — not a regional manager three states away."

### 8. "I'm under contract/non-compete"
> "Most real estate 'non-competes' are about client lists and proprietary data, not about where you hang your license. Let's look at your specific agreement. In Texas, overly restrictive non-competes for independent contractors are very difficult to enforce. But I always recommend following the terms of your agreement — and we're happy to wait."

### 9. "I just moved to my current brokerage"
> "I understand the hesitation. You don't want to be that agent who moves every year. Here's what I'd suggest: let me show you the numbers now. File it away. If in 6 months you're still happy, great. If you're starting to feel the fees, you'll already know what the alternative looks like."

### 10. "Spyglass isn't big enough / I've never heard of you"
> "179 agents and growing fast. But here's the real question: how much business do you get from your brokerage's brand name? If the answer is less than the $15K you'd save by switching, the brand isn't worth what you're paying for it. Our agents close deals because of their skills and our tools — not a logo on a sign."

### 11. "What about E&O / insurance?"
> "We handle E&O just like any other brokerage. It's built into our fee structure — no extra charge. You're covered from day one."

### 12. "Can you match my current deal?"
> "I don't need to match it — I need to BEAT it. Run your numbers through our Calculator. For the vast majority of productive agents, our model wins. And if for some reason it doesn't at your specific volume, I'll tell you honestly."

### 13. "I want a signing bonus / lead guarantee"
> "We don't do signing bonuses because we'd rather put that money into tools that help you every day for years, not a one-time check. Agents who chase signing bonuses often find that the brokerage recoups it in fees over time. We'd rather just give you a great deal from day one."

### 14. "I'm thinking about going independent"
> "I respect that entrepreneurial mindset. Let me ask: have you calculated the cost of E&O, MLS dues, tech tools, office space, marketing, and back-office support? Most independents spend $20-40K/year on overhead. At Spyglass, your total cost after cap is around $12K + $285/transaction. You get everything an independent would build — plus 179 agents for referrals and collaboration — for less than doing it alone."

### 15. "The market is bad — I don't want to change now"
> "Actually, a shifting market is the BEST time to change. When the market is hot, the brokerage doesn't matter — everyone's making money. In a tough market, the commission you keep and the tools you have make the difference between surviving and thriving. Spring is coming, rates are trending down — the agents who position themselves NOW will win when demand returns."

## Closing the Conversation

### The Soft Close
> "Look, I'm not going to pressure you. You're a professional. You know your business. All I'm asking is: run the numbers. If Spyglass can save you ${{SAVINGS}} a year AND give you better technology AND a growing team around you — doesn't that deserve a serious look?"

### The Direct Close
> "Based on everything we've talked about, you'd save ${{SAVINGS}}/year, you'd get access to Mission Control and Pulse, and you'd join a team that's growing fast in the right direction. What would it take to make this happen?"

### The Timeline Close
> "Spring market is here. Listings are picking up. If you're going to make a move, doing it now means you start your next listing cycle with the savings. Every month you wait is another month of paying fees you don't need to pay."
