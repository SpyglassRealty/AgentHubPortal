# Spyglass Realty Website Audit
## Copy, Design & User Experience — February 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Spyglass Realty has a solid foundation: genuine testimonials, real results, and a strong value proposition. But the website doesn't match the caliber of the brokerage. The copy reads like it was written for search engines, not humans. The design feels 2018. The user experience is functional but forgettable. For a brokerage that claims "Design is Paramount" as a core value, the site needs to live up to that promise.

**The gap:** You're a boutique, high-touch brokerage competing with firms that have Compass-level branding. Your agents and results are elite. Your website doesn't communicate that yet.

## I. COPY AUDIT

### 🔴 Critical Issues

**1. The copy talks AT people, not TO them.**
Almost every page uses formal, third-person corporate language. "Our group of the best Austin real estate agents has helped over 3,000 families achieve the dream of homeownership." This is billboard copy, not web copy. In 2026, consumers expect conversational, direct language that makes them feel seen.

**Fix:** Shift to second-person, emotional, problem-aware copy.
- Before: "Our mission is to help Austin home sellers and buyers by raising the bar in real estate service."
- After: "Selling your home shouldn't feel like a second job. We handle everything — staging, marketing, negotiations — so you can focus on what's next."

**2. Self-congratulatory tone erodes trust.**
The site says "best" 14+ times across pages. "Best Austin real estate agents," "best real estate company in Austin," "the best realtors in Austin." Consumers are numb to superlatives. The more you say you're the best, the less they believe it.

**Fix:** Let results speak. Replace claims with specifics:
- Instead of "best real estate agents" → "Agents who've closed 3,000+ homes and earned 700+ five-star reviews"
- Instead of "world-class marketing" → "Every listing gets professional video, drone footage, staging, and a targeted ad campaign reaching 50,000+ local buyers"

**3. Outdated/inaccurate claims on the site.**
- Homepage still says "We offer staging and remodeling for FREE" — per your notes, this is no longer offered.
- Services page says "complimentary staging services, renovation coordination" — same issue.
- "Over 400 5-star reviews" on services page vs "Over 700" on homepage. Pick one (it should be 700+).
- References to "Ryan's wife" Sunny for staging — needs updating if no longer applicable.

**4. SEO-stuffed copy hurts readability.**
Phrases like "home buyers Austin," "Austin TX realtors," "real estate Austin brokerage" are jammed in unnaturally. Google's algorithms have evolved — they reward natural language now. The keyword stuffing makes the copy feel robotic and damages trust.

**5. The homepage headline is weak.**
"Highly Reviewed, Trained, and Experienced Real Estate Agents in Austin" — this is a description, not a value proposition. It tells me what you ARE, not what you DO FOR ME.

**Better:** "Your Home. Our Obsession." or "We don't just list homes. We launch them." or "700+ families trusted us with their biggest investment. Here's why."

**6. Testimonials are walls of text.**
The testimonials page is a massive scroll of unformatted paragraphs. No photos of the reviewers, no neighborhood tags, no sorting, no video integration. Some testimonials are clearly transcribed from video (conversational/rambling) and need editing for readability.

### 🟡 Moderate Issues

**7. Repetitive testimonials across pages.**
The same 6-8 testimonials appear on the homepage, services page, buying page, selling page, AND testimonials page. This creates a "small pond" feeling. With 700+ reviews, there should be unique testimonials on each page, curated to match the page's context (buyer testimonials on the buying page, etc.).

**8. Weak calls-to-action.**
CTAs are generic: "Speak to a Consultant," "Start Your Real Estate Journey," "Get Started." These don't create urgency or communicate value.

**Better CTAs:**
- "Get Your Free Home Valuation" (selling page)
- "See Homes Before They Hit the Market" (buying page)
- "Find Out What Your Home Is Really Worth" (homepage)
- "Schedule a 15-Minute Strategy Call" (contact)

**9. The "What brings you here today?" section is hollow.**
Two image cards (Buying/Selling) with no description, no benefit, no hook. This is prime real estate on the homepage — it should immediately validate the visitor's intent and give them a reason to click.

**10. No clear differentiator in the first 5 seconds.**
A visitor landing on the homepage sees: Austin skyline photo, "Highly Reviewed..." headline, search bar. This could be any of 500 Austin real estate sites. Within 5 seconds, they should know: (1) what makes Spyglass different, (2) what you'll do for them, and (3) why they should trust you.

### 🟢 What's Working

- **Testimonials are genuine and detailed** — real names, specific outcomes (sold in 3 days, got asking price, record deal for neighborhood)
- **The credibility bar** (Inc 5000, Austin Business Journal, RealTrends, Leading RE) is effective
- **The selling page** is the strongest page on the site — it addresses specific pain points and has a clear structure
- **FAQ content on buying page** is genuinely helpful

## II. DESIGN AUDIT

### 🔴 Critical Issues

**1. The design feels dated (2018-era).**
Heavy use of the Spyglass orange (#EF4923), dark backgrounds, stock-feeling photography, and generic section layouts. It looks like a premium WordPress theme from the late 2010s. Award-winning design in 2026 is characterized by: generous whitespace, subtle animations, editorial typography, and intentional restraint.

**Reference sites that are winning awards:**
- Compass.com (clean, editorial, whitespace-driven)
- side.com (modern tech-meets-real-estate)
- redfin.com (data-forward, clean UI)
- Douglas Elliman (luxury editorial feel)

**2. Typography needs a major upgrade.**
The current heading font (appears to be a decorative serif) fights with the body copy. Modern real estate sites use either:
- A strong geometric sans (like Inter, Söhne, or Graphik) for a tech-forward feel
- An elegant serif (like Freight Display or Canela) for luxury positioning
Currently, you have an inconsistent mix that doesn't commit to either.

**3. Photography is the weakest element.**
- Homepage hero: Austin skyline at dusk — generic, could be any city's real estate site
- Stock-feeling team photos scattered throughout
- No lifestyle imagery of actual Austin neighborhoods
- No photos of real staged homes that Spyglass has done

**Fix:** Invest in:
- Editorial-quality photography of your actual staged listings (before/after)
- Neighborhood lifestyle photos (people at Barton Springs, South Congress, etc.)
- Candid team photos that feel authentic, not corporate
- Client photos paired with their testimonials

**4. The credibility bar is good but needs better execution.**
The logos (Inc 5000, ABJ, RealTrends, Leading RE) run together visually. They should breathe. Consider a subtle animation where they fade in, or a dedicated "As Recognized By" section with better spacing.

**5. Orange overload.**
The Spyglass orange is a strong brand color, but it's used everywhere — buttons, headings, backgrounds, testimonial sections, footer. It creates visual fatigue. Use orange as an accent (CTAs, key highlights) and let the rest of the design breathe with whites, light grays, and your dark navy.

### 🟡 Moderate Issues

**6. The testimonial carousel on the homepage is hard to read.**
Long testimonial text in a slider that auto-advances is a UX anti-pattern. Users can't read fast enough. Replace with a curated grid of 3-4 short, punchy testimonials with photos, or a video testimonial highlight reel.

**7. The "Reasons to Work with Us" section is underwhelming.**
Numbered list with paragraph text — looks like a textbook. These should be visual cards with icons and one-sentence value props, not paragraphs.

**8. Core Values page has layout inconsistencies.**
Images are different sizes, some left-aligned, some right-aligned, with inconsistent spacing. The "SELLING WITH US" screenshot image at the bottom looks like a placeholder. The page needs a cohesive grid system.

**9. Contact page is bare.**
Just a form with four fields. No map, no office photos, no team photo, no "What to expect" section, no reassurance. This is the page where trust needs to be highest.

**10. Mobile experience needs attention.**
The homepage is very long on mobile — the testimonial carousel is especially problematic, and the hero section doesn't communicate value quickly enough on a small screen.

### 🟢 What's Working

- **Dark header/footer** creates a professional frame
- **The Spyglass logo** is clean and recognizable
- **The selling page layout** with alternating image/text sections is effective
- **Orange as a brand color** is distinctive and energetic — just needs to be used more sparingly

## III. USER EXPERIENCE AUDIT

### 🔴 Critical Issues

**1. No social proof "above the fold."**
The first thing visitors should see (after your value prop) is proof: "700+ 5-star reviews" with a Google/Zillow star rating widget, or a short video testimonial. Currently, you have to scroll through 4+ sections before seeing any testimonials.

**2. The homepage journey is confusing.**
The page goes: Hero → Buying/Selling cards → "Helping You Unlock..." → "Realize your Dream..." → Reasons to Work with Us → Testimonials → 700 Reviews → "New Form of Realty" → CTA. This doesn't follow a logical persuasion framework.

**Better flow:**
1. Hero with clear value prop + social proof (700+ reviews, 3000+ homes)
2. Buying/Selling path selection (with benefit copy)
3. Your unfair advantage (what makes you different — staging, marketing, negotiation)
4. Proof (2-3 testimonials with photos + video)
5. How it works (simple 3-step process)
6. Credibility (awards, press, numbers)
7. CTA (consultation form embedded)

**3. No video on the homepage.**
Video is your secret weapon — you have video testimonials and you mention video marketing as a service. Yet the homepage has zero video. A 60-second "Why Spyglass" video above the fold would dramatically increase engagement and time on page.

**4. Multiple pages say the same thing.**
Services, Core Values, Why Choose Spyglass, and the homepage all cover similar ground with slightly different copy. This is confusing for users and dilutes your message. Consolidate into a single, compelling "Why Spyglass" narrative.

**5. The "Dashboard" button in the nav is confusing for new visitors.**
It's an IDX login for existing clients but it appears prominently in the main navigation alongside "Get a Consultation." New visitors don't know what it is and may click it expecting something else.

### 🟡 Moderate Issues

**6. No "How It Works" section anywhere.**
Buying and selling are complex. A simple 3-step visual process would reduce anxiety and increase conversions:
- Step 1: Free consultation
- Step 2: We create your custom plan
- Step 3: We execute while you relax

**7. No trust badges or security indicators near forms.**
Contact form has "*Your information will never be shared" in small text. Modern best practice includes visible trust badges, a brief privacy assurance, and social proof near the form.

**8. Client Video Testimonials page is nearly empty.**
Just a list of names with no video thumbnails, no preview text, no way to filter. The videos appear to require JavaScript rendering that doesn't work on crawl. This should be your most engaging page.

## IV. SPECIFIC RECOMMENDATIONS (Priority Order)

### Phase 1 — Quick Wins (1-2 weeks)
1. **Fix inaccurate copy** — Remove "free staging" claims, update review count to 700+
2. **Rewrite the homepage headline** — Make it about the consumer, not about you
3. **Add social proof above the fold** — Star rating + review count + logos
4. **Upgrade CTAs** across all pages to be specific and value-driven
5. **Curate testimonials by page** — Different testimonials on each page, matching context

### Phase 2 — Design Refresh (4-6 weeks)
6. **Homepage redesign** following the persuasion flow above
7. **Reduce orange** — Use it only for CTAs and key accents
8. **New photography** — Real listings, real neighborhoods, real team
9. **Typography upgrade** — Commit to a type system that reflects positioning
10. **Video integration** — Homepage hero video, testimonial video grid

### Phase 3 — Structural Changes (6-8 weeks)
11. **Consolidate redundant pages** — One "Why Spyglass" narrative
12. **Add "How It Works"** section to buying and selling pages
13. **Redesign testimonials page** — Photo grid, filtering, video integration
14. **Redesign contact page** — Add office photos, map, team, "what to expect"
15. **Build a proper "About/Story" page** — Ryan's story, the founding, the mission

## V. THE NORTH STAR

The site should feel like walking into a boutique hotel, not a convention center. Every element should communicate: *"We are obsessively good at what we do, and we'll bring that same obsession to your home."*

The copy should make someone feel understood before they fill out a form. The design should make them feel like they're already working with someone premium. And the experience should make them think: *"These people are different."*

Because you are.
