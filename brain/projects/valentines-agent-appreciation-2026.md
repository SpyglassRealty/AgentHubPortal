# 💝 Valentine's Day Agent Appreciation

## Why This Matters
- Retention IS recruiting. Every agent who stays is one you don't have to replace.
- Valentine's Day = perfect excuse for a genuine "we appreciate you" message
- 179 agents → 300 goal means retention is 50% of the equation
- Personal touches from the broker/owner hit different than generic corporate emails

## Email Template (Ready to Send)

**Subject:** We love having you at Spyglass 💛

---

Hey [First Name],

Happy Valentine's Day from the Spyglass family.

I know this sounds corny — a Valentine's from your broker — but I've been thinking about something. When I started Spyglass, I wanted to build a brokerage where agents actually *wanted* to be. Not because of a contract or a golden handcuff, but because it felt like the right place.

You're here. That means something to me.

Whether you closed 50 deals last year or you're still building your book, you're part of what makes this brokerage different. I see the work you put in. The late-night showings, the tough negotiations, the clients you go above and beyond for.

So — thank you. For trusting us. For being part of this.

Here's to a monster 2026 together. 🏠

Ryan

P.S. — If there's anything I can do to help you have your best year yet — tools, support, training, whatever — just reply to this email. I read every one.

---

## Why This Email Works
1. **Personal voice** — Sounds like Ryan, not a marketing department
2. **Acknowledges all production levels** — "Whether you closed 50 deals or you're building"
3. **No ask** — Pure appreciation, no CTA to buy/do something
4. **The P.S.** — Opens a door for agents who are struggling (retention risk) to reach out
5. **Short** — Respects their time
6. **The anti-corporate flex** — "Not because of a contract or a golden handcuff" subtly highlights Spyglass's no-BS culture vs. franchise brokerages

## Suggested Delivery
- **Send from:** Ryan's personal email (ryan@spyglassrealty.com), NOT a mass marketing tool
- **Timing:** Feb 15 morning (Saturday — agents check email casually, more likely to read)
- **Alternative:** If Ryan has a company Slack or group text, a shorter version there works too
- **Follow-up:** Track who replies to the P.S. — those are your most engaged agents
