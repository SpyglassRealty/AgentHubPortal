### Growth Trajectory
- Q4 2024: 24,140 agents → Q1 2025: 26,870 → Q3 2025: 30,183
- Adding ~1,000 agents/month organically
- First $1B revenue year in 2024; on pace for ~$2.3B in 2025
- **6x agent growth since end of 2021**

### Revenue Share Model (Why Agents Join Real)
- **5-tier revenue share** — sponsors earn up to $4,000/year per tier 1 agent
- Tiers: 5% → 4% → 3% → 2% → 1% of referred agent's cap ($12,000)
- **Co-sponsorship:** Two agents can split sponsorship credit (50/50)
- **Willable revenue share:** After 5 years producing, revenue share passes to heirs
- **Retirement plan:** After 5 years, earn 100% of rev share even out of production
- Cap: $12,000/year at 85/15 split, then 100% to agent
- **Stock grants** for agents (NASDAQ: REAX)
- **Real Wallet Capital:** Working capital loans against future commissions

### Private Label Program (Spyglass's Model)
- Keep Spyglass brand 100% — "truly private labels" are invisible
- Access Real's full tech stack (reZEN), equity, healthcare, revenue sharing
- Available in Texas and 19+ other states
- Options: "As Is" brand, "Powered By", or Co-Brand
- Spyglass joined January 2025, nearly doubling Real's Austin agent count

### AI & Technology Investments
- **Leo:** AI assistant handling 47% of all agent support calls via reZEN (up from 28% in Q2)
- **Dedicated automation team** launched Q3 2025 — "reduce manual or low-value processes"
- **Acquired Flyhomes AI search portal** — integrating AI-powered home search
- **Real Wallet Capital** — fintech lending product for agents
- **reZEN platform** — proprietary end-to-end platform (transactions, compliance, financials)

### What This Means for Spyglass
- **Leverage Real's scale** for tech, compliance, and financial products
- **Revenue share** as a recruiting advantage (agents earn passive income by referring)
- **Private Label** lets Spyglass keep its boutique brand while accessing enterprise infrastructure
- **Leo AI** reduces support burden — Spyglass can supplement with Mission Control's custom tools
- **Stock grants** are a unique retention tool vs traditional brokerages

## 2. The Compass-Anywhere Mega-Merger

### What Happened
- **Compass acquired Anywhere Real Estate** for **$1.6B** (all-stock deal)
- Closed **January 9, 2026** — faster than anyone expected
- Creates the **world's largest brokerage** by volume
- Brands absorbed: Coldwell Banker, Century 21, Sotheby's International Realty, ERA, Better Homes and Gardens RE

### Scale of the Combined Entity
- Combined: 50,000+ agents across premium and franchise brands
- Market share dominance in luxury and high-volume segments
- CEO Ryan Schneider (Anywhere) departed post-merger

### Impact on Austin Market
- Compass already had significant Austin presence
- Coldwell Banker has legacy Austin market share
- **Consolidation creates recruiting opportunity:** Disgruntled agents from absorbed brands will be looking for new homes
- Real's CEO Tamir Poleg: "Once that transaction closes, we will likely see a little bit more interest from people who are searching for a new path"

### Spyglass Opportunity
- Agents from Coldwell Banker/Century 21 in Austin who don't want to be "absorbed" into Compass
- Spyglass + Real offers: boutique feel + enterprise tech + revenue share + equity
- **Key message:** "We're not a mega-corp. We're a tech-forward boutique that gives you what the big guys can't — personal attention AND cutting-edge tools."

## 3. Industry-Wide Brokerage Consolidation Trends

### T3 Sixty 2026 Trends Report — Key Findings
- **Top 10 US brokerages** now control **27.2%** of sales volume (up from 14.3% in 2017)
- **27 of the top 100 brokerages from 2018** have been acquired
- Two growth models dominating:
  1. **M&A:** Compass, Peerage, HomeServices
  2. **Organic platforms:** eXp (#3), Real (#5), Redfin (#8), Side (#9), LPT Realty (#16)

### The Platform Brokerages
| Brokerage | 2025 Rank | Growth Model |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| eXp Realty | #3 | Rev share + equity + virtual offices |
| The Real Brokerage | #5 | Rev share + equity + reZEN platform |
| Redfin | #8 | Portal + lead gen + technology |
| Side | #9 | Back-office platform for top teams |
| LPT Realty | #16 | Rev share + low fees |
| Fathom Realty | #18 | Low flat-fee model |

### Key Insight
- **None of the platform brokerages own/lease offices** (except where required by law)
- Capital that would go to offices → agent compensation, tech, growth
- LPT Realty hit $10B in sales in just **3 years** using this model
- Speed of organic growth is "staggering" per T3 Sixty

### What This Means for Spyglass
- Spyglass is part of the #5 largest brokerage in the US (Real)
- But competes as a **boutique brand** in Austin — best of both worlds
- The "office-less" trend means traditional brokerages are increasingly disadvantaged
- Spyglass's tech investments (Mission Control, Pulse, IDX) differentiate from other Real Private Label teams

## 4. Brokerage Leader Sentiment (2026)

### HousingWire Survey (Feb 2026)
- **82%** of brokerage leaders expect transaction sides to rise in 2026
- **66%** expect to gain market share
- Confidence is high despite 2025 headwinds

### Recruiting Intelligence
- **Mid-performing agents** are the most likely to move (not the unhappy ones — the stuck ones)
- **High performers** switch when growth plateaus
- **Team leaders** move when operational support lags
- Timing matters more than compensation for recruiting
