## Executive Summary

Austin is deep in buyer's market territory entering Q1 2026. Prices are soft, inventory is elevated, and homes are sitting longer than at any point since 2011. However, mortgage rate stabilization in the low 6% range and the potential for Fed cuts through mid-2026 could trigger renewed buyer activity in H2 2026. **This is a recruiting window** — agents at weaker brokerages are feeling the squeeze, and Spyglass's lead gen + brand power is a compelling pitch.

## What This Means

### For Buyers
- **Best buying conditions since 2019-2020** — significant negotiating power
- Homes under $350K still competitive (limited inventory in this segment)
- Price stabilization expected Q3-Q4 2026 → buying now locks in lower prices
- **Key talking point**: A home that peaked at $550K in 2022 is now ~$430K with lower rates coming

### For Sellers
- Must price right from day one — 53% taking price cuts
- Staging, marketing, and agent quality matter more than ever
- **Spyglass advantage**: 1,000+ reviews = trust = buyer eyeballs = faster sales

### For Agents
- Agents are doing fewer deals at lower prices = income compression
- Weaker brokerages cutting back on support, leads, marketing
- **Recruiting angle**: "Your volume is down. Are you paying more in fees for less support?"
