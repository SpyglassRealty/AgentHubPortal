6 Creeks is a new master-planned community located in the city of Kyle, Texas, one of the fastest-growing communities in Texas that offers plenty of family activities, amenity centers, trails, playgrounds, and sports fields. This new community is situated 25 miles from Downtown Austin, 12 miles from San Marcos, and hours away from San Antonio. 6 Creeks offers tranquil living and family-friendly amenities to its residents with convenient access to shopping, dining, and recreational attractions in Austin. 

Homes for sale in 6 Creeks are built and designed by the top home builders in the nation, Coventry Homes, Highland Homes, M/I Homes, and Trendmaker Homes.

M/I Homes offers 11 different floor plans with a game room or a two-story single-family home with size of 1,644 to 2,815 square feet. All new homes in 6 Creeks include a granite countertop in the kitchen and bathrooms, 42" custom-made cabinets, unique exterior features, a walk-in shower, covered back patios, plus many other designs. Each new home from Coventry Homes is built with a comprehensive Eco Smart technology for eco-friendly living and smart home automation technology for today's modern living.

For over 50 years, Perry Homes built a sterling reputation as one of the most trusted builders in Texas. Perry Homes offers move-in ready and available to build homes with smart home technology and an industry-leading new home warranty with homes that are designed to cater to a variety of lifestyles. Trendmaker Homes has built a reputation for raising homeowners' expectations and their commitment to improving the quality of life. Each new Trendmaker home features a LivingSmart® program to promote health, energy-efficiency, and well-being. Highland Homes is dedicated to providing the best possible home building and home buying experience.

6 Creeks is served by Hays CISD that offers rigorous instruction with a commitment to serve the unique need of every student. Negley Elementary School, Barton Middle School, and Hays High School are the schools within the neighborhood. For those on higher education, the University of Texas and Texas State University are just a short drive from the community.
