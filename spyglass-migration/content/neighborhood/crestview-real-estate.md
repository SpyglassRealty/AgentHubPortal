## About Crestview

Crestview feels like a small town, although it's just a few miles north of downtown Austin. The neighborhoods attract couples, families, single young professionals, students, and seniors.The Urban Patchwork relies on volunteers to plant crops in local gardens and share them with homeowners. Residents also support school plays, fundraisers, and clean-up and community events. The residents maintain a strong community spirit and stay close to the area's farming roots.
Crestview residents have access to major parks.
Also, the North Austin Optimist Club holds a lease on a baseball and softball field for local youth teams. Austin's commuter rail train, which opened in 2010, has a station in Crestview on its way between [downtown Austin](/downtown-austin.php)and [Leander](/leander-homes-for-sale.php), far to the northwest. Next to the station a business/residential center called Midtown Commons has been built. The neighborhood also has several Austin public bus lines leading to downtown or the university campus.

Homes for sale in Crestview are usually small with about 1,400 square feet that were built in the 1940s to the 1960s. Due to the desirable location of the neighborhoods, home values have steadily increased.
