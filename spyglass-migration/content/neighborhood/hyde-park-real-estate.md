## About Hyde Park Neighborhood

Hyde Park is an old, historic neighborhood in Austin, Texas. It is located close to [downtown Austin](/downtown-austin.php) and the University of Texas - Austin. Hyde Park is one of the most desirable places to live in because of its safety, sense of belonging in the community and the convenient location for the residents. There are plenty of amenities in the neighborhoods that are walking distance from homes.

The neighborhood itself stretches from 38th Street to the 45th and Guadalupe to Duval and is located to the north of the University of Texas - Austin campus. Students populate the area due to its proximity to the UT campus.

Most residents in the area live an active lifestyle because of the green spaces that are within a walking distance, neighbors are close to each other, and are very friendly due to the neighborhood's small-town vibe. Hyde Park has a strong sense of community and houses plenty of singles, young and old families, and young professionals.

Two major local thoroughfare border the neighborhood. These are Red River St. and Guadalupe St., also known as "The Drag". Shops and restaurants are mainly located within these areas. Everything is accessible to the people living in Hyde Park, whether you are looking for retail stores, grocery store, restaurants, cafes, bars, bakeries, fitness centers, theaters, galleries, and more, you'll be able to find it in just a few minutes of walking, driving, or even a bike ride!

Public transportation is accessible to the residents since many of them rely on public buses and rail.

The area has mostly single-family homes, bungalows, with diverse styles. Students in the area are usually in apartments and areas that are not too far from the center of the neighborhood. Homes in Hyde Park range from $250 up to $1M.

### Schools in Hyde Park

Students in Hyde Park may attend the following schools located within or near the area: Stepping Stone School for Preschool, Ridgetop Elementary School for K-5, Hyde Park Middle School for Grades 6-8, and Huntington-Surrey School for High School.
